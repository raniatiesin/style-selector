# Business Overview

## THE INSIGHT THAT STARTED THIS

Most great business thinkers on Substack are prisoners of text. They write brilliantly. They have real experience. They have paying subscribers who love them. But their reach is capped by the format — Substack is a relatively small corner of the internet, and written content doesn't travel the way video does.

The content is already done. The ideas are already sharp. The writing is already edited and polished. What's missing is a single distribution channel: short-form video. Remove that bottleneck and the audience grows — by definition.

That's the entire business premise. Not "let me help you make content." But "your content already exists and is being underserved. Let me fix that."

---

## THE OFFER

**What I do:** Turn a newsletter writer's existing posts into short-form faceless videos optimized for Reels, Shorts, and TikTok.

**What I don't do:** Write scripts from scratch, invent ideas, manage accounts, run ads, or touch anything that isn't directly taking their words and making them visual.

**Why faceless:** Lower production barrier. Faster to produce. Scales without needing a face on camera. The content is the star, not the presenter — which is exactly right for idea-driven business content.

**The core value proposition:** More paid subscribers. Not "stunning visuals." Not "great content." More subscribers. That's the only outcome that matters to a paid newsletter writer.

## THE IDEAL CLIENT (ICP)

→ Full breakdown: [[ICP and Niche]] · [[Customer Profile]]

**Who they are:** A business founder or operator who built or ran something real — a company, a consultancy, a product — and is now sharing the mechanics of how they did it through a paid Substack newsletter. Think Hormozi-style: frameworks, real numbers, first principles, zero fluff. They write from lived experience, not theory.

**The critical tone filter:**

- [w] "Here's how I got my first 100 clients with no budget" → aspirational, teachable, TikTok-native audience
- [-] "Here's what I'd tell a fellow operator about CAC" → peer-to-peer, LinkedIn-native audience

**The numbers:** "Thousands of paid subscribers" on Substack — roughly 1,000–5,000 paid. Enough traction to care about growth. Small enough to not have a team handling this already.

**The profile in plain language:** Content-rich and video-poor. Posts are structured and idea-first — frameworks, numbered principles, real stories with specific lessons. Authority comes from track record and ideas, not personality. Zero short-form video presence anywhere.

**Who they are NOT:**

- [-] Marketing or growth experts (they'd see through any pitch and already know the tools)
- [-] AI or tech creators (already experimenting with video tools themselves)
- [-] Self-help, mindset, or "escape the rat race" writers (personality-first content, wrong format)
- [-] Finance or investing newsletters (different audience, different trust model)
- [-] Peer-to-peer operator writers whose readers are other operators (LinkedIn audience, not TikTok)
- [-] Anyone with hundreds of thousands of subscribers (too established, already have teams)
- [-] Anyone you recognize by name (if you know them, they're probably too big)
- [-] Writers already active on TikTok, Instagram, or any short-form platform (no distribution gap)

No website. No case studies. No polished brand. None of that matters yet.

1. Find newsletter writers who match the ICP exactly
2. Send them to tiesin.me via cold outreach

## OUTREACH — WHAT WORKS AND WHAT DOESN'T

→ Full detail: [[Outreach]]

**Dead channel:** Substack's native DM. Notifications are buried, inbox is never checked. Do not use as primary outreach.

**Live channels (in order of priority):**

- **Email** — contact email on their about page or personal site. Primary channel. Gets read.
- **Newsletter reply** — subscribe, read the newsletter the moment it arrives, reply to it as a real reader. Lands directly in their personal inbox, not cold.
- **Twitter/X** — reply to a tweet first, then DM. Gets seen.
- **LinkedIn** — especially effective for the founder/operator ICP.

**Current email setup:**

- Sending from raniatiesin@gmail.com during warmup period
- tiesin.me domain warming up — switch over at week 3
- Never send from a cold domain without warmup or you land in spam

## NEWSLETTER REPLY TACTIC

→ Full detail: [[Outreach]]

Subscribe to target writers. The moment a newsletter arrives, read it and reply as a real reader. This is not cold outreach — it lands in their personal inbox and comes with reader context.

**Structure:** reaction → bridge → offer

- **Reaction:** One specific thing from the newsletter you actually engaged with. Not a compliment. A real response — "the point about X contradicts what most people say about Y — and you're right."
- **Bridge:** One sentence connecting their idea to video naturally.
- **Offer:** Brief. They know you exist as a reader so it needs less setup.

**The test:** If you removed the offer, would the reply still make sense to send? If yes — the reaction is real. If no — you wrote the reaction to serve the pitch. Start over.

This tactic works at low volume only. Each reply requires actually reading and thinking.

## PRICING

→ Commission mechanism: [[Commission Tracking]]

Two tiers. Commission is tracked via Substack's native referral program — the writer generates a unique referral link, it goes in every video description, attributed subscribers are visible to both parties on the referral leaderboard in real time. Payment settled monthly via invoice based on verified count.

**The Bet** — $0/mo + 40% commission on attributed paid subscriber revenue. Pure performance. For writers who won't pay upfront.

**The Partnership** — $200/mo + 20% commission on attributed paid subscriber revenue. Ten videos a month. $20/video. Writer needs 15 new subscribers to break even. Flat fee covers model costs only — real income is commission compounding over time.

**The Engine** — $1,000/mo flat, zero commission. For writers who prefer no commission arrangement. Presented as a secondary option, not the featured tier. Exists to anchor the Partnership price — shows the commission is worth ~$800/mo in cash terms, implying $4,000/mo subscriber revenue.

The free video comes before either tier. No commitment until they've seen proof.

**Why the subscriber count floor exists for commission to work:** Commission is 20% of paid subscriber revenue. A writer with 100 total subscribers is making $0–$50/month. 20% of that is $10 — not enough to justify 10 videos of production. Commission only works if the writer has a base of paying subscribers already. The 1,000 paid minimum is not arbitrary — it protects the business model.

## HOW I FIND THE RIGHT PEOPLE

→ Full system: [[Prospecting]] · [[Prospecting — Edge Copilot Method]]

**Platform:** Substack, starting here before expanding to Medium, Patreon, and others.

**The filter:** "Thousands of paid subscribers" is the target label. Anything less is too small. "Tens of thousands" or more is too established.

**Search terms that find the right writers:**

- `founder lessons`
- `bootstrapped`
- `what I learned building`
- `one person business`
- `solopreneur`
- `ran a business`
- `business mistakes`
- `behind the business`
- `operator notes`
- `founder diary`

**How to qualify fast — the 6-question filter:**

Run these in order. Stop at the first No.

1. Does it say "Thousands of paid subscribers"? ✅
2. Is their content structured and idea-driven (not personal diary)? ✅
3. Do they write *for* aspiring builders — aspirational, mechanics-first, teachable? ✅
4. Do they write about real business experience (not marketing, AI, mindset, self-help)? ✅
5. Zero short-form video presence anywhere? ✅
6. Can I find a single strong idea in their last 3 posts? ✅

All six yes = send them to tiesin.me.

**Questions 1 and 5 are instant disqualifiers.** Any red on either — stop immediately, don't read further.

## QUALIFICATION VALUE SYSTEM

→ Full system: [[Prospecting]]

### 1. Subscriber count

- [w] **Thousands of paid subscribers** — 1K–9.9K paid. Target.
- [?] **Hundreds of paid subscribers** — Below floor but growing. Borderline.
- [d] **Tens of thousands** — Too established. Likely has a team.
- [-] **No paid tier / unknown** — Not monetized. Skip.

### 2. Content structure

- [w] **Frameworks, numbers, steps** — Clear visual beats. Can storyboard in 60 seconds.
- [?] **Mix of structured + narrative** — Some posts work, some don't.
- [d] **Mostly narrative / reflective** — Hard to turn into video.
- [-] **Personal diary / emotional** — Wrong format entirely. Skip.

### 3. Writes for aspiring builders

- [w] **Aspirational, teachable, mechanics-first** — "Here's how I did it." TikTok audience.
- [?] **Mixed — some aspirational, some peer** — Proceed with caution.
- [d] **Peer-to-peer with aspirational moments** — Audience mostly operators.
- [-] **Peer-to-peer operator content** — LinkedIn audience. No gap. Skip.

### 4. Niche

- [w] **Business mechanics / founder story** — Built something real, teaching it.
- [?] **Adjacent — ops, leadership, product** — Could work. Read closely.
- [d] **Semi-adjacent — sales, strategy** — Borderline. High risk of wrong ICP.
- [-] **Marketing / AI / self-help / finance / HR** — All traps. Skip immediately.

### 5. Video presence

- [w] **Zero — nowhere** — Full distribution gap. Ideal.
- [?] **Inactive — old posts, no recent content** — Gap partially open. Acceptable.
- [d] **LinkedIn video only** — TikTok/Reels gap still open. Borderline.
- [-] **Active on TikTok / Reels / Shorts** — No gap. Skip immediately.

### 6. Strong idea in last 3 posts

- [w] **Yes — clear hook, specific lesson** — Can storyboard it in 60 seconds.
- [?] **One strong post out of three** — Workable. Lower confidence.
- [d] **Vague ideas, hard to visualize** — Production risk. Likely skip.
- [-] **No — abstract, emotional, or theoretical** — Can't make the video. Skip.

## THE TRAPS TO AVOID

→ Full breakdown: [[ICP and Niche]]

- [!] **The famous name trap.** If you know their name, they're too big. The right clients are unknown to you today.
- [!] **The algorithm trap.** Substack's discovery surfaces popular content — too established, too marketing-adjacent, or too self-help. Fight it with specific search terms and manual filtering.
- [!] **The AI solopreneur trap.** Most saturated content category online. Those writers are already experimenting with AI video tools. They don't need you.
- [!] **The self-help trap.** Sounds adjacent to the right niche. It's not. Motivational and personality-first — doesn't cut into faceless video well.
- [!] **The peer-to-peer operator trap.** Founders writing for other founders have a LinkedIn audience, not a TikTok one. You need writers whose readers *want* to be them, not writers whose readers *are* them.
- [!] **The already-on-video trap.** If the writer is already active on TikTok or Instagram, there is no distribution gap. Nothing to fill. Skip immediately.
- [!] **The small subscriber trap.** A writer with under 1,000 paid subscribers hasn't validated their own offer yet. Commission math doesn't work. Hold the filter — it protects your time.
- [!] **The perfection trap.** No website, brand, pricing page, or case study is needed before the first client. The first client produces the case study. Do it in order.
