## THE 6-QUESTION FILTER

Run in order. Stop at the first No. **Questions 1 and 5 are instant disqualifiers** — any fail on either, close the tab.

| #  | Question                                                                              | Pass |
| :- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :--- |
| 1  | Does it say "Thousands of paid subscribers"?                                          | ✅    |
| 2  | Is their content structured and idea-driven (not personal diary)?                     | ✅    |
| 3  | Do they write *for* aspiring builders — aspirational, mechanics-first, teachable?     | ✅    |
| 4  | Do they write about real business experience (not marketing, AI, mindset, self-help)? | ✅    |
| 5  | Zero short-form video presence anywhere?                                              | ✅    |
| 6  | Can I find a single strong idea in their last 3 posts?                                | ✅    |

All six yes = send them to tiesin.me.

## QUALIFICATION VALUE SYSTEM

Use when filling your prospect tracking table. Apply column 1 and 5 first — they are instant disqualifiers.

### 1. Subscriber count

- [w] **Thousands of paid subscribers** — 1K–9.9K paid. Target.
- [?] **Hundreds of paid subscribers** — Below floor but growing. Borderline.
- [d] **Tens of thousands** — Too established. Likely has a team.
- [-] **No paid tier / unknown** — Not monetized. Skip.

### 2. Content structure

- [w] **Frameworks, numbers, steps** — Clear visual beats. Can storyboard in 60 seconds.
- [?] **Mix of structured + narrative** — Some posts work, some don't.
- [d] **Mostly narrative / reflective** — Hard to turn into video.
- [-] **Personal diary / emotional** — Wrong format entirely. Skip.

### 3. Writes for aspiring builders

- [w] **Aspirational, teachable, mechanics-first** — "Here's how I did it." TikTok audience.
- [?] **Mixed — some aspirational, some peer** — Proceed with caution.
- [d] **Peer-to-peer with aspirational moments** — Audience mostly operators.
- [-] **Peer-to-peer operator content** — LinkedIn audience. No gap. Skip.

### 4. Niche

- [w] **Business mechanics / founder story** — Built something real, teaching it.
- [?] **Adjacent — ops, leadership, product** — Could work. Read closely.
- [d] **Semi-adjacent — sales, strategy** — Borderline. High risk of wrong ICP.
- [-] **Marketing / AI / self-help / finance / HR** — All traps. Skip immediately.

### 5. Video presence

- [w] **Zero — nowhere** — Full distribution gap. Ideal.
- [?] **Inactive — old posts, no recent content** — Gap partially open. Acceptable.
- [d] **LinkedIn video only** — TikTok/Reels gap still open. Borderline.
- [-] **Active on TikTok / Reels / Shorts** — No gap. Skip immediately.

### 6. Strong idea in last 3 posts

- [w] **Yes — clear hook, specific lesson** — Can storyboard it in 60 seconds.
- [?] **One strong post out of three** — Workable. Lower confidence.
- [d] **Vague ideas, hard to visualize** — Production risk. Likely skip.
- [-] **No — abstract, emotional, or theoretical** — Can't make the video. Skip.

## WHAT ZERO RESPONSES FROM 20 SENDS ACTUALLY MEANS

Zero responses is not enough data to know if copy or targeting is the problem. But after reviewing the prospect list: **the targeting was the primary issue.** The writers contacted were marketing-adjacent, self-help-adjacent, or below the subscriber floor — exactly the wrong ICP.

The algorithm trap was active: Substack surfaces popular content, which skews toward marketing, personal development, and peer-to-peer operator content. Manual filtering with specific search terms is required to escape it.

**The copy also had problems** (fixed in the current version above). But fixing copy on a broken targeting list would have produced the same result.

Fix order: targeting first, then copy, then volume.

- `founder lessons`
- `bootstrapped`
- `what I learned building`
- `one person business`
- `solopreneur`
- `ran a business`
- `business mistakes`
- `behind the business`
- `operator notes`
- `founder diary`

Fight the algorithm. These terms are specific enough to cut through.

## POST TITLE PATTERNS — RIGHT vs WRONG

### Right — mechanics-first, aspirational, TikTok audience

- [w] "How I got my first 100 clients with no budget — the exact sequence"
- [w] "We went from $0 to $40K/month in 8 months — here's the breakdown"
- [w] "The exact hiring mistake that almost killed my company"
- [w] "How I Break Down a Problem Before Solving It"

### Wrong — marketing, peer-to-peer, emotional, abstract

- [-] "Brand Strategy ≠ Design Strategy ≠ Marketing Strategy" — marketing
- [-] "Why selling is not a coincidence" — sales/marketing adjacent
- [-] "Executive Authority: The Anti Personal Brand" — personal branding, peer-to-peer
- [-] "The Hidden Risk of Over-Niching" — marketing strategy
- [-] "The Logic of Dread: Why Your Sense of Foreboding is a Business Tool" — psychology framing
- [-] "Off Hours: On Bootstrapping and the Danger of Comparison" — comparison = mindset content

The title alone usually tells you. If you'd see it on a LinkedIn thought leader's profile, skip.
