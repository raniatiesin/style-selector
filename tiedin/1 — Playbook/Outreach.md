## THE OUTREACH STACK

**Dead channel:** Substack native DM. Notifications are buried. Inbox is never checked. Never use as primary channel.

**Live channels in priority order:**

1. **Newsletter reply** — subscribe, read on arrival, reply as a real reader. Highest trust, lowest friction. Lands in personal inbox.
2. **Email** — contact email from their about page or personal site. Primary cold channel.
3. **Twitter/X** — reply to a tweet first, then DM. Gets seen.
4. **LinkedIn** — effective for the founder/operator ICP.

## COLD EMAIL COPY

### Working version (March 20, 2026)

> **Subject:** Free video from your writing

[Name],

Your piece on [specific idea] is exactly the kind of structured idea that travels on short-form video — the readers who'd pay for this aren't on Substack, they scroll.

I want to build one from it. Faceless, branded to you, from your own words. Free, in your inbox in 2 days.

Two minutes: [link]

— Rania

### Why this version

- First line earns the offer — gives them a reason before the ask
- Does not pre-handle objections (reads as salesy)
- Centers what they're missing, not what you want to do
- Short because the setup does the work

### What was rejected and why

**Version that led with compliment:** "Your writing deserves a bigger room" — every cold pitch opens with flattery. They feel it in sentence one and stop reading.

**Offer-first without setup:** Announcing the free video before giving a reason lands flat. They haven't accepted the premise yet.

**"I want to build the first one for you"** — centers you. "I want" is your agenda showing.

## NEWSLETTER REPLY TACTIC

Subscribe to target writers. The moment a newsletter arrives in your inbox, read it and reply as a real reader. This bypasses cold entirely — it lands in their personal inbox and you have reader context.

### Structure

**reaction → bridge → offer**

**Reaction:** One specific thing from the newsletter you actually engaged with. Not a compliment. A real response. "The point about X contradicts what most people say about Y — and you're right."

**Bridge:** One sentence connecting their idea to video naturally.

**Offer:** Brief. They know you as a reader. Less setup needed than cold.

### The test

If you removed the offer, would the reply still make sense to send? If yes — the reaction is real. If no — you wrote the reaction to serve the pitch. Rewrite.

### Limitation

Only works at low volume. Each reply requires actually reading and thinking. Do not template this.
