# ICP and Niche

**Related:** [[Business Overview]] · [[Customer Profile]] · [[Prospecting]]

---

## WHY BUSINESS MECHANICS IS THE RIGHT NICHE

Business mechanics content — frameworks, real numbers, step-by-step processes, founder stories with specific lessons — is naturally visual. You can put steps on screen. You can animate a sequence. You can show a number moving. The content already has structure; the video just makes that structure visible.

The reader of this content wants to *execute*. They follow to learn and apply. When a good video surfaces a new writer to them, they subscribe because there's more to learn. That's subscriber growth — the only metric that matters to a paid newsletter writer.

Business mechanics writers have zero short-form video presence because translating structured, idea-dense content into compelling video requires skill. That difficulty is the moat. It's why no one has done it for them yet — and why there is no competition with the right ICP.

---

## WHY EVERY OTHER NICHE FAILS

- [-] **Self-Help / Inner Work / Mindset** — The content is internal and experiential. There is no visual equivalent of a feeling. The audience consumes this content to *feel understood*, not to execute. High watch time, zero subscriber conversion. These writers already have video presence — therapy-speak and aesthetic emotional content is native to Instagram and TikTok. The gap does not exist here. Wrong format. Wrong audience intent. No gap to fill.

- [-] **Marketing / Growth / Audience Building** — These writers already understand distribution. They've considered short-form video and many are already experimenting with it. The pitch — "let me show you what's possible" — lands flat when they already know what's possible. Audience sees through the pitch. No asymmetry.

- [-] **AI / Tech Solopreneur** — The most saturated content category online. These writers are the earliest adopters of AI video tools — they are building and testing those tools themselves. You are offering something they are already doing or will do within weeks. They don't need you. They are you, six months ahead.

- [-] **Finance / Investing** — Financial content is built on credentials, compliance, and personal authority. It does not travel well as faceless video — the audience needs to trust the source specifically. Content skews toward analysis and commentary, not mechanics and frameworks. Wrong trust model. Wrong content structure.

- [-] **Peer-to-Peer Operator Content** — Founders writing *for* other founders. The tone is insider, peer-level, LinkedIn-native. The reader is not an aspiring builder — they are already building. Their social platform is LinkedIn, not TikTok. A video made from this content would land in a feed where nobody in the audience lives. Wrong audience platform. No distribution gap.

## THE TONE FILTER — THE MOST IMPORTANT DISTINCTION

Two writers can cover the exact same topic and one is right and one is wrong. The difference is tone.

- [w] **RIGHT:** "Here's how I got my first 100 clients with no budget — the exact sequence I used." Aspirational. Teachable. Mechanics-first. Written *for* someone who wants to build something. That reader is on TikTok.

- [-] **WRONG:** "Here's what I'd tell a fellow operator about early customer acquisition." Peer-to-peer. Insider. Written *at* someone who's already building. That reader is on LinkedIn.

The content topic is identical. The audience is completely different. The video distribution gap only exists for the first one.

## THE CONTENT STRUCTURE TEST

Before committing to a writer, ask: can I turn one post into a video with clear visual beats?

**Good signs:**

- [w] Numbered frameworks ("The 3 things I did before I quit my job")
- [w] Real numbers with context ("We went from $0 to $40K/month in 8 months — here's the breakdown")
- [w] Step-by-step processes with a clear beginning and end
- [w] Specific stories with a named lesson at the end

**Bad signs:**

- [-] Abstract emotional concepts ("learning to sit with discomfort")
- [-] Reflective essays without a clear takeaway action
- [-] Metaphors and analogies as the primary vehicle
- [-] Content that requires the writer's voice or face to land

If you can't storyboard a post in 60 seconds, the content is wrong for faceless video.

---

## THE DISTRIBUTION GAP — WHY THIS WORKS AT ALL

The entire business premise is one sentence: **the right readers for this content are on TikTok and Instagram, not on Substack.**

A business founder sharing real mechanics has a natural audience of people who want to build something real. That audience scrolls. They don't browse Substack. They will never find this writer unless the writer goes to them — and going to them means video.

The writer can't do it themselves because:

1. They don't have video skills
2. They don't have the time
3. They don't know how to translate their written ideas into short-form structure

That's the offer. The gap is real, the problem is real, and the writers who fit the ICP feel it — they just haven't been approached by someone who can solve it with a finished product rather than a pitch.

---

## DISQUALIFIERS — SKIP IMMEDIATELY IF:

- [!] The writer's post titles are emotional or introspective ("why I stopped chasing success")
- [!] The content is about how to *feel* rather than what to *do*
- [!] You recognize the writer's name (too big, already has a team)
- [!] They already have any short-form video presence — TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- [!] Their Substack subscriber count shows anything other than "Thousands of paid subscribers"
- [!] Their readers appear to be other operators, not aspiring builders
- [!] The niche is marketing, AI, self-help, spirituality, finance, or HR

---

## THE RIGHT NICHE IN ONE SENTENCE

A business founder with real operating experience, teaching the mechanics of how they built something to an audience that wants to build the same — with 1,000–5,000 paid subscribers, zero video presence, and content that is structured, specific, and visually translatable.

Content-rich. Video-poor. That's the entire filter.
