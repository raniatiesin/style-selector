# Commission Tracking

**Related:** [[Business Overview]] · [[Website Structure]]

---

## THE MECHANISM

Substack has a built-in referral program. When a writer activates it, every subscriber gets a unique referral link. Anyone who subscribes through that link is tracked and attributed to the referrer automatically — no manual counting, no disputes.

You are treated as a referrer. The writer gives you your unique referral link before any video goes live. That link goes in the description of every video posted.

Every new subscriber who clicks through is logged on the writer's referral leaderboard. The count is visible to both you and the writer in real time.

---

## THE COMMISSION STRUCTURE

- You take 20% of the paid subscription revenue generated by subscribers attributed to your referral link
- Attribution is verified through the Substack referral dashboard — the number is the same for both parties
- Payment is settled monthly based on confirmed attributed subscribers

---

## WHY THIS IS CLEAN

- [w] No self-reporting — Substack tracks it, not you or the writer
- [w] No ambiguity — the leaderboard shows the exact count
- [w] No disputes — both parties see the same number from the same source
- [w] No external tools needed — the referral program handles everything natively

---

## WHAT NEEDS TO HAPPEN AT ONBOARDING

- [ ] Writer activates Substack referral program (if not already on)
- [ ] Writer generates a unique referral link for you
- [ ] You add that link to every video description before posting
- [ ] Both parties agree on monthly payment based on attributed subscriber revenue

That's it. Four steps. Everything after that is automatic.
